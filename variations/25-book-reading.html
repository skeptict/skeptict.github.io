On the Typography of Books

The Twenty-Eighth of December, 2025
There is something timeless about the printed page. The careful selection of typeface, the generous margins, the texture of paper beneath one's fingers—these elements combine to create an experience that digital media struggles to replicate. Book-inspired design brings these principles to the screen: serif fonts for extended reading, ample line spacing for comfort, and a color palette reminiscent of aged paper and ink. The goal is not merely to imitate books, but to capture their essence—the quiet dignity, the invitation to slow down and read deeply.

In Praise of the Drop Cap

The Twenty-Fifth of December, 2025
The drop cap is a humble flourish with ancient roots, dating back to illuminated manuscripts. This single enlarged letter serves as both decoration and signal—here begins something worth reading. In web design, such typographic details create rhythm and visual interest without resorting to images or complex layouts. They remind us that text itself can be beautiful, that letters are not merely vehicles for meaning but objects of aesthetic contemplation in their own right.
